Key Projects

1. Faster Firmware Updates (2023–2024)
I found myself standing in front of a real problem: devices in the field could only be updated via Zigbee, and it took 2.5–4 hours to update a 200-device network. Even worse, it required gateways—additional hardware the customer might not have. When bugs surfaced, we were stuck.

I designed and implemented a Bluetooth-based update system that works with any mobile phone. No gateways. No special infrastructure. Just your phone and a device.

Result: Update time dropped to ~25 minutes from ~4 hours. Operational costs plummeted. Troubleshooting went from "weeks" to "hours." This feature ships in all new Signify sensor devices now. It's not glamorous work, but it has impact.

2. SDK Modernization & Integration
Signify had fragmented SDKs across multiple product lines—each with its own quirks, versioning headaches, and maintenance burden. Scaling became painful. Every new product release required re-inventing wheels.

I took ownership of the entire intake process: evaluated SDK versions, identified breaking changes, designed a modular architecture, and ensured the transition didn't introduce performance regressions or memory bloat. I made it repeatable for future upgrades.

Result: Zero regression in performance or memory usage. A process that can scale to dozens of products. This is work that doesn't make headlines, but it makes the entire organization more efficient.

3. Platform Unification (Ongoing)
Signify maintains multiple hardware platforms and product lines with overlapping capabilities. The result: maintenance overhead, support complexity, and slower product launches. We need to consolidate without breaking thousands of devices already in the field.

I'm architecting a unified firmware platform that consolidates multiple product lines while maintaining backward compatibility and seamless data migration. It's a multi-year effort, but it's the right architectural decision for where the company is heading.

Learning: Large-scale system design isn't just about the code. It's about stakeholder alignment, technical risk management, and thinking three years ahead.

Master's Internship & Research (2022–2023)

During my final year of masters, I completed a 6-month research internship that became the foundation for much of my work at Signify. I was given a challenge: develop a way to measure network performance in large-scale Zigbee deployments holistically.

I built an algorithm and testing framework that tracks reliability, robustness, and responsiveness with quantitative metrics. The result: automated "health reports" for deployed networks. This directly solved a real pain point at Signify—ensuring that SDK upgrades and firmware changes don't quietly degrade performance in the field. It's a tool that's still used today in our validation pipeline.

Valencia, 2024
I presented this research at the PIMRC International Conference in Valencia—and this picture represents something I'm proud of. I went on my own dime despite presenting on behalf of Signify, because I believed in the work and wanted to contribute to the research community. It was an investment in myself and in pushing the boundaries of what I could do. That conference gave me visibility, credibility, and connections that have opened doors ever since.
